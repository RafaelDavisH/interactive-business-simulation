What is a Problem Hypothesis?
A falsifiable statement about who your customer is, what problem they have, and when it occurs. It guides your discovery interviews and can be proven wrong; that's the point.

What Makes a Good Interview Question
Good questions ask about past behavior and specific events, not future hypotheticals. They never pitch your idea or lead the respondent toward a particular answer. Unbiased questions surface real problems; leading questions only confirm your assumptions.

TAM → SAM → SOM Framework
TAM (Total Addressable Market) = entire market if 100% captured.   SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) = segment you can realistically target.   SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) = realistic near-term capture. Investors care most about how you derived SOM.

What is a Smoke Test?
A smoke test (or "fake door" test) measures real customer interest before building anything. A landing page + ad campaign can validate demand in days, not months. Key metric: email capture rate above 5% is a strong signal.
